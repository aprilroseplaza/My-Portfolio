About me

As a child, I believed in the power of education, which inspired me to pursue it no matter the challenges. My love for being around children grew stronger as I matured, shaping my passion for teaching. Becoming a teacher has been a fulfilling journey, as I believe that through guidance and education, a teacher has the power to change lives.

I believe that education is the key to unlocking a student's full potential, empowering them to think critically, explore creatively, and grow with confidence. My approach to teaching is centered on creating an engaging and supportive learning environment where every student feels valued and inspired to reach their dreams. Through patience, passion, and dedication, I strive to not only impart knowledge but also to nurture a lifelong love for learning.

Fun fact:

I have a love for dancing and watching movies, which bring me joy and creativity. Reading books fuels my curiosity and broadens my knowledge, while my passion for science experiments keeps me constantly exploring new ways to make learning engaging and exciting.

Education:

I recently earned my degree from Holy Cross of Davao College, where I cultivated my passion for teaching and continuous learning. Throughout my academic journey, I actively participated in school events and volunteer opportunities, allowing me to grow both personally and professionally.

Experience:

I have gained valuable teaching experience through multiple classroom and laboratory demonstrations, which have enhanced my skills in delivering engaging and effective lessons. Each experience has strengthened my ability to adapt, communicate, and inspire students in their learning journey.

Leadership:

I had the privilege of serving as the Public Information Officer, a role that honed my communication skills and leadership abilities.

My ability to integrate technology enhances the learning experience, allowing me to engage students with innovative tools and resources that support their academic growth. Additionally, I have a solid foundation in curriculum design, tailoring lessons to meet diverse learning styles and ensuring content is both relevant and challenging to foster critical thinking and creativity.

I designed a lesson plan focusing on how landforms and bodies of water influence typhoons, specifically within the Philippine Area of Responsibility (PAR). This is an engaging and relevant topic that helps students understand the impact of geographical features on weather patterns. In this lesson, I guide my students in analyzing and identifying the areas where a typhoon is likely to make landfall within the PAR, enhancing their geographic and meteorological skills.

I use Google Meet to create interactive virtual learning experiences, ensuring students remain engaged and connected.
